Features

Balanced Response Across Frequencies

Regular bookshelf speakers require a subwoofer to handle lower frequencies. Flat speakers provide consistent and balanced bass, mid, and high frequencies, so you don’t need additional equipment to enjoy high-quality sound.

Sound Clarity

Sound is naturally produced by vibrations that move air around an instrument. Our flat panels replicate this process by creating vibration regions on the panel. This method closely mimics the original sound source. Bookshelf speakers rely on a cone or diaphragm to produce sound, which can limit accuracy.

Sound Coverage

Cone speakers deliver the best sound when the listener is directly in front of them, often requiring multiple speakers to cover a room. Our Flat Speakers, however, can project sound up to 170 degrees from a single panel, providing expansive coverage and easily filling the room with one (mono) or two (stereo) flat speakers.

Sound Reach and Attenuation

Flat speakers excel in maintaining sound quality over longer distances compared to bookshelf speakers. Cone speakers often attenuate lower frequencies over distance, leading to a high-pitched sound. Flat speakers preserve the full range of frequencies, ensuring consistent sound quality.

Minimum Room Interactions

Unlike bookshelf speakers, which can cause sound reflections and resonance within a room, our flat panels have minimal interaction with the room environment. Their design reduces unwanted reflections, providing a clearer sound experience.

Bidirectional Speaker

Flat Speakers are ideal for musicians, churches, pubs, and live performers because they emit sound from both the front and back of the panel. This creates an almost 360-degree sound experience, eliminating the need for separate monitors.

Portable

Weighing just 2 kg, our speakers are perfect for on-the-go listening, providing high-quality sound without taking up much space in your vehicle.

No Mic Feedback

Flat Speakers are a great choice for live performances because they are inherently immune to feedback sounds caused by microphones or instruments. This ensures a smooth and uninterrupted audio experience.
